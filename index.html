Psychology MCQs - Class 12

  
I. Foundations of Psychological Attributes
  
    
1. What is the definition of a psychological attribute?
    
A) A physical characteristic of a person.
B) A quality or trait that is related to the mind.
C) A person's social interactions.
D) The environment surrounding an individual.
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: Psychological attributes are traits related to mental and emotional functioning.
  
  
    
2. Which of the following is classified as a psychological attribute?
    
A) Height
B) Weight
C) Intelligence
D) Hair color
    
Answer: C
    
Explanation: Intelligence is a cognitive trait, unlike physical attributes.
  
  
    
3. The concept of "individual differences" in psychology refers to:
    
A) The similarities among people.
B) Variations in psychological attributes.
C) Influence of situations on behavior.
D) Assessment of physical traits.
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: Individual differences highlight unique psychological traits.
  
  
    
4. What does the term "situationism" suggest about behavior?
    
A) Behavior is determined by internal traits.
B) Greatly influenced by external circumstances.
C) Solely genetic.
D) Random and unpredictable.
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: Situationism focuses on the role of environment in shaping behavior.
  
  
II. Assessment Methodologies in Psychology
  
    
5. What is the main purpose of psychological assessment?
    
A) Measure physical traits.
B) Evaluate psychological qualities.
C) Analyze social systems.
D) Predict future life events.
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: It evaluates attributes like intelligence and personality.
  
  
    
6. Which of the following is a common method used in assessment?
    
A) Physical exams
B) Psychological tests
C) Economic indicators
D) Historical records
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: Standardized psychological tests measure mental traits.
  
  
    
7. What does "self-report" refer to in psychological assessment?
    
A) External observation
B) Individual’s own report
C) Algorithmic report
D) Public data
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: Self-reports collect data directly from individuals.
  
  
    
8. What are key concerns in using psychological tests?
    
A) Cost and time
B) Reliability and validity
C) Test administrator's appearance
D) Popularity of the test
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: Tests must be consistent (reliable) and accurate (valid).
  
  
III. The Concept and Nature of Intelligence
  
    
9. According to the PDF, intelligence involves:
    
A) Accumulating wealth
B) Understanding things
C) Winning arguments
D) Controlling others
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: Intelligence involves comprehension and reasoning.
  
  
    
10. Which of the following describes intelligence?
    
A) Adjusting to new situations
B) Predicting future
C) Object manipulation
D) Mind reading
    
Answer: A
    
Explanation: Adaptability is a core component of intelligence.
  
  
    
11. Intelligence also involves the ability to:
    
A) Solve problems
B) Memorize lists
C) Run fast
D) Paint
    
Answer: A
    
Explanation: Problem-solving is central to intelligence.
  
  
    
12. Intelligence is NOT:
    
A) Understanding
B) Adjustment
C) Solving
D) Flying
    
Answer: D
    
Explanation: Intelligence refers to mental, not physical, capabilities.
  
  
    
13. David Wechsler defined intelligence as:
    
A) Storing information
B) Acting purposefully, thinking rationally, dealing effectively
C) Reacting quickly
D) Expressing emotions
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: His definition highlights action, thought, and adaptation.
  
  
    
14. "Acting purposefully" means:
    
A) Random behavior
B) Goal-directed action
C) Emotional reaction
D) Instinctive behavior
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: Acting with intent toward objectives shows intelligence.
  
  
    
15. "Thinking rationally" refers to:
    
A) Illogical decisions
B) Sensible, logical choices
C) Impulsivity
D) Ignoring outcomes
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: Rational thinking uses logic for good judgment.
  
  
    
16. Sternberg suggests intelligence:
    
A) Means knowing everything
B) Doesn’t mean knowing everything
C) Can be precisely measured
D) Is fixed and unchangeable
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: Sternberg broadens intelligence beyond just knowledge.
  
  
IV. Historical and Theoretical Perspectives
  
    
17. Who proposed the Unifactor Theory?
    
A) Charles Spearman
B) Louis Thurstone
C) Alfred Binet
D) Arthur Jensen
    
Answer: C
    
Explanation: Binet believed in a single general intelligence.
  
  
    
18. A central idea in Binet’s theory:
    
A) Multiple independent abilities
B) One general ability
C) Fluid and crystallized intelligence
D) Practical skills
    
Answer: B
    
Explanation: Intelligence is a single factor affecting performance.
  
  
    
19. Binet described intelligence as the ability to:
    
A) Judge well
B) Understand well
C) Reason well
D) All of the above
    
Answer: D
    
Explanation: These are the mental abilities central to Binet's view.
  
  
20. Spearman's Two-Factor Theory includes:
  
A) Only one factor
B) Two types of abilities - general and specific
C) Seven intelligences
D) No intelligence factors
  
Answer: B
  
Explanation: Spearman proposed a general intelligence (g) and specific abilities (s).
  
21. Thurstone proposed:
  
A) Emotional intelligence
B) Theory of multiple intelligences
C) Primary Mental Abilities
D) g and s factors
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: Thurstone suggested intelligence is composed of several primary mental abilities.
  
22. Gardner’s theory of multiple intelligences includes:
  
A) Only academic skills
B) Eight distinct intelligences
C) IQ and EQ
D) Analytical and creative
  
Answer: B
  
Explanation: Gardner proposed eight different intelligences like linguistic, musical, bodily, etc.
  
23. Emotional intelligence includes:
  
A) Only IQ
B) Ability to solve math problems
C) Managing and understanding emotions
D) Memory skills
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: Emotional intelligence involves recognizing, understanding, and managing emotions.
  
24. IQ stands for:
  
A) Intelligence Question
B) Intelligent Quality
C) Intelligence Quotient
D) Intellectual Quiz
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: IQ means Intelligence Quotient, used to measure intellectual abilities.
  
25. Which formula is used for IQ?
  
A) (MA/CA) × 100
B) (CA/MA) × 10
C) MA + CA
D) CA ÷ MA
  
Answer: A
  
Explanation: IQ = (Mental Age ÷ Chronological Age) × 100.
  
26. The average IQ score is:
  
A) 50
B) 100
C) 120
D) 80
  
Answer: B
  
Explanation: An average IQ is generally considered to be 100.
  
27. Intelligence tests are primarily used to:
  
A) Test emotions
B) Improve memory
C) Assess mental abilities
D) Evaluate handwriting
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: Intelligence tests help evaluate an individual's cognitive abilities.
  
28. Creativity is best defined as:
  
A) Memorizing facts
B) Logical thinking
C) Ability to generate novel ideas
D) High IQ
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: Creativity refers to the ability to think of original and innovative ideas.
  
29. Divergent thinking means:
  
A) Thinking in one direction
B) Memorizing
C) Multiple possible solutions
D) Copying ideas
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: Divergent thinking involves exploring many possible solutions.
  
30. Convergent thinking means:
  
A) Thinking broadly
B) Multiple answers
C) Focusing on one correct solution
D) Guessing randomly
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: Convergent thinking leads to a single, correct answer.
  
31. Creativity can be improved by:
  
A) Repetition
B) Punishment
C) Encouraging imagination
D) Limiting expression
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: Creative thinking thrives in environments that support imagination and freedom.
  
32. Aptitude tests measure:
  
A) Past achievements
B) Emotions
C) Potential to learn
D) Handwriting speed
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: Aptitude tests assess an individual's capacity to acquire new skills.
  
33. Intelligence is:
  
A) Only academic knowledge
B) Capacity to solve problems
C) Memorization skill
D) High grades
  
Answer: B
  
Explanation: Intelligence involves problem-solving, reasoning, and adapting to new situations.
  
34. Which of these is NOT part of Gardner's intelligences?
  
A) Musical
B) Linguistic
C) Emotional
D) Bodily-Kinesthetic
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: Emotional intelligence is not one of the original multiple intelligences proposed by Gardner.
  
35. The ability to learn from experience defines:
  
A) Talent
B) Memory
C) Intelligence
D) Attention
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: Intelligence includes the ability to learn, adapt, and apply knowledge.
  
36. Which test is used to measure creativity?
  
A) Binet Test
B) Torrance Test
C) Wechsler Test
D) Stanford Test
  
Answer: B
  
Explanation: Torrance Tests of Creative Thinking are widely used to assess creativity.
  
37. Intelligence is affected by:
  
A) Heredity only
B) Environment only
C) Both heredity and environment
D) Neither
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: Both genetic factors and environmental conditions influence intelligence.
  
38. Who introduced the concept of IQ?
  
A) Alfred Binet
B) David Wechsler
C) William Stern
D) Charles Spearman
  
Answer: C
  
Explanation: William Stern introduced the IQ formula.
